  • How to communicate

    We must speak in a form of a form - neither dangerous nor fast - with the letters in a form of a form and not a short line in it, and it is not beautiful for it to be an easy sentence.

  • How to communicate

    The degree of hearing varies from one person to another, and it is possible to speak loudly but without shouting, because in the case of shouting, the face loses its features, so the deaf person cannot read the face.

  • How to comunicate

    We have to ensure that the deaf person's attention is not distracted, for example, we cannot attract their attention and give them information at the same time. We must wait until the deaf person is able to see the texts, images, or objects, and then we explain what we want to tell hi

  • Their number in countries and their number of languages

    Statistics from the World Federation of the Deaf stated that there are about 72 million deaf people around the world, 80 percent of whom live in developing countries, and use more than 300 sign languages

  • Their types

    Physiological mutism: is the physiological inability to speak or make sounds. Selective mutism: It is a psychological disorder in which a person is unable to speak in anxiety-inducing situations, but is otherwise able to speak. Hearing muteness is the condition of not using speech as a means of communication due to deafness. This may or may not be a choice. Preferred terms include: non-verbal, non-vocal or mute.
